DOCUMENT 1: INTERNAL CREDIT POLICY MANUAL - RISK FRAMEWORK
=============================================================================
1.1 DEBT-TO-INCOME (DTI) STANDARDS
The bank enforces a baseline rule regarding debt-to-income limits. The maximum recommended DTI for any standard individual application is capped strictly at 45%. Any expansion past this baseline creates immediate risk classification spikes.
Exception Provision: The credit committee allows a conditional variance extending permissible DTI thresholds up to 50% exclusively for applicants identified within the Medium risk profile bucket, provided clear verification parameters (e.g., secondary guarantor, steady alternate asset backing) are successfully delivered. Any candidate displaying a debt footprint over 55% DTI is labeled an automatic Reject.

1.2 COLLATERAL AND VALUE RATIOS
For all secured products, the maximum allowed Loan-to-Value (LTV) ratio is capped at 80% for Tier-1 real estate holdings and 70% for alternate assets. Residential property appraisals must originate from a verified bank panel valuer. 

1.3 CO-APPLICANT CONDITIONS
A primary wage-earning co-applicant must be declared if the primary applicant's unadjusted credit assessment drops into the High-risk category. The financial history of the co-applicant will be parsed with identical scrutiny.

DOCUMENT 2: REGULATORY GUIDE - RBI MASTER CIRCULAR ON LOANS & ADVANCES
=============================================================================
2.1 STATUTORY EXPOSURE BASICS
Per Central Bank directives, a bank’s aggregate exposure framework to a single counterparty entity must never exceed 15% of the total capital funds available within the banking institution. Tier-1 capital reserves govern this validation threshold.

2.2 IDENTIFICATION OF NON-COOPERATIVE BORROWERS
A borrower is officially designated as "Non-Cooperative" if they possess the clear financial capacity to settle recorded debt constraints but intentionally withhold payments, misapply disbursed fund parameters, or neglect to provide mandatory corporate transparency records.

2.3 PROVISIONING HIGHLIGHTS FOR DEFAULTS
Assets classified as Non-Performing Assets (NPAs) demand immediate, systematic regulatory ledger adjustments:
- Sub-Standard Assets (Defaulting <= 12 months): Require a base uniform provision allocation of 15%.
- Doubtful Assets (Defaulting > 12 months): Require provision asset brackets scaling rapidly from 25% up to 100% based on exact validation durations of unsecured exposure metrics.

DOCUMENT 3: COMPLIANCE CODE - FAIR PRACTICES LENDING FRAMEWORK
=============================================================================
3.1 TRANSPARENCY PRINCIPLES IN RATE MANAGEMENT
The institution must provide a clean Interest Rate Disclosures matrix to every applicant. Loan sanction agreements must explicitly itemize structural component items including the processing fee, floating index rules, reset periods, and prepayment penalties.

3.2 NON-DISCRIMINATORY SELECTION NORMS
Lending evaluations must follow strict objective criteria. Applications cannot be handled differently based on demographic criteria or regional background parameters. Decisions must rest entirely upon risk-score parameters, asset validation, and document clearance metrics.

3.3 GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL PROTOCOLS
Applicants have a regulatory right to dispute risk decisions. If a customer files a complaint regarding service delivery or evaluation bias, the Grievance Redressal Officer must respond with a formal resolution layout within a maximum structural period of 21 calendar days.
